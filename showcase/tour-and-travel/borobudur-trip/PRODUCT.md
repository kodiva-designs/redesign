# Product Context

## Register

Brand (marketing landing page — design IS the product)

## Product Purpose

Portfolio showcase demonstrating landing page design craftsmanship. Presents Borobudur temple (Central Java, Indonesia) as a premium editorial destination experience.

## Users

**Primary:** Design recruiters, hiring managers, and potential clients reviewing portfolio work.

**Secondary:** Fellow designers seeking inspiration.

**User Goals:**
- Evaluate visual design skills and aesthetic judgment
- Assess frontend craftsmanship (layout, typography, color, responsiveness)
- See how design systems translate to real-world travel/hospitality contexts

## Brand Identity

**Brand Name:** Borobudur Trip (portfolio piece, not a real operator)

**Tone:**
- Editorial and refined
- Cinematic and evocative
- Calm and confident
- Never promotional or salesy

**Voice:**
- Third-person observational (like a travel journal)
- Present tense for immediacy
- Short, precise sentences
- No exclamation marks

## Strategic Principles

1. **Editorial over promotional** — this is a destination journal, not a booking engine
2. **Photography leads** — imagery carries the narrative; text supports
3. **Restraint is luxury** — whitespace, lightweight typography, minimal UI chrome
4. **Authentic mood** — golden hour, morning mist, architectural detail, human scale
5. **One focal point per section** — never compete for attention

## Narrative Angle

"Stone and Light" — architectural and visual storytelling focusing on:
- Borobudur's sculptural artistry (2,672 relief panels, 72 stupas)
- Dramatic Javanese landscape (volcanoes, rice terraces, morning mist)
- Cinematic quality of the site at dawn
- Scale and timelessness

## Anti-References

Avoid these aesthetics at all costs:
- SaaS / startup visual language
- Glassmorphism or blur effects
- Neon gradients or dark-mode futurism
- AI-generated travel imagery
- Influencer-style photography
- Corporate blue palettes
- Gamification patterns
- Loud, aggressive CTAs
- Pinterest-boho clichés
- Dribbble-inspired visual tricks
- Over-layered interfaces
- Trendy 3D illustrations

## Success Criteria

The design succeeds if:
- It feels like a beautifully art-directed destination publication
- It does NOT feel like a tourism website or booking platform
- Typography hierarchy is clear and elegant
- Color palette feels derived from the landscape
- Each section has editorial pacing (like magazine spreads)
- The hero creates immediate emotional immersion
- A recruiter would停留 to explore the full page

## Content Strategy

**Imagery:** Pexels stock photography — Borobudur temple, Javanese landscapes, volcanic scenery, architectural details. Golden hour lighting, wide environmental framing, muted earth tones.

**Copy:** Minimal. Every word earns its place. No filler paragraphs, no restated headings.

**Data:** Authentic-feeling statistics (visitor counts, UNESCO designation year, temple dimensions) for the Trust section.
