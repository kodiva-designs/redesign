# Homepage Guideline: Riverie

## 1. Page Role

The homepage is the digital arrival experience for Riverie. It should immediately communicate that Riverie is a modern tropical retreat in Bali: calm, architectural, open-air, natural, and quietly premium.

The page must balance two needs:

1. **Create desire:** Let visitors feel the atmosphere through space, photography, slow copy, and material detail.
2. **Create confidence:** Help visitors understand what Riverie offers, who it is for, what rooms or retreats are available, where it is located, and how to book.

The homepage should not behave like a generic hotel booking template. It should feel editorial and immersive, while still making the booking path obvious.

---

## 2. Primary Users

The homepage should serve:

- Couples looking for a quiet Bali stay.
- Solo travelers seeking calm and comfort.
- Remote workers or long-stay guests who want a peaceful base.
- Small groups who want shared spaces with a boutique-hotel feeling.
- Design-aware travelers who care about architecture, materiality, and atmosphere.
- Local and international guests who need clear booking and contact information.

Their main job to be done:

> Help me understand the feeling of the place, decide if it fits my trip, compare stay options, and book or inquire without confusion.

---

## 3. Homepage Narrative

The homepage should move like a slow visit through the property.

1. **Arrive:** See the pool, architecture, light, and calm.
2. **Understand:** Learn what Riverie is in one clear sentence.
3. **Consider:** Explore rooms, amenities, and shared spaces.
4. **Imagine:** See moments: morning coffee, open-air dining, poolside stillness, quiet rooms.
5. **Trust:** Understand location, details, policies, contact, and booking path.
6. **Act:** Check availability or inquire.

---

## 4. Recommended Sitemap Context

The homepage should point to these key destinations:

- Stay / Retreats
- Room Detail
- Experience
- Journal
- About
- Contact
- Booking / Inquiry

Recommended main navigation:

**Stay / Experience / Journal / About / Contact / Book Now**

**Stay / Experience / About / Contact / Book Now**

## 5. Homepage Section Blueprint

## Section 01: Hero Arrival

### Purpose

Create immediate emotional recognition. The user should know this is calm, tropical, modern, and premium within the first screen.

### Recommended Layout

- Full-width architectural/pool image or split layout with image on the right and copy on the left.
- Use a calm hero image: pool, palm trees, open-air dining, or architectural facade.
- Keep text area quiet and readable.
- Avoid placing small text over busy areas of the photo.

### Content

**Kicker:**

MODERN TROPICAL RETREAT IN BALI

**Headline options:**

- Slow living, naturally.
- A modern tropical retreat in Bali.
- Stay where the day moves slower.
- Open-air calm, considered by nature.

**Body copy option:**

Riverie is a quiet tropical retreat shaped by open-air architecture, warm natural textures, and thoughtful spaces for resting, gathering, and lingering a little longer.

**Primary CTA:**

Book Now

**Secondary CTA:**

Explore the Retreat

### Design Notes

- Use Playfair Display for the headline.
- Use Manrope for body copy and buttons.
- CTA should be visible above the fold.
- Avoid autoplay video unless it is silent, optimized, and does not delay the page.

---

## Section 02: Booking Bar

### Purpose

Give ready-to-book visitors an immediate path without making the homepage feel commercial.

### Recommended Layout

- Horizontal bar on desktop, stacked card on mobile.
- Place below hero or lightly overlapping the lower hero area if contrast remains clean.
- Keep the module flat, with Limestone border and Coconut White background.

### Fields

- Check-in
- Check-out
- Guests
- Room Type, optional
- Check Availability button

### States

- Empty state
- Focus state
- Selected dates
- Unavailable dates
- Loading
- Error
- Confirmation

### Copy Rules

Use clear labels. Do not use placeholder-only fields.

### Avoid

- Urgency countdowns.
- Fake scarcity.
- Flash sale banners.
- Crowded third-party booking UI styling.

---

## Section 03: Brand Intro

### Purpose

Explain what Riverie is in a short, elegant, practical way.

### Recommended Layout

- Editorial two-column layout.
- Left: small label and headline.
- Right: concise paragraph and optional detail list.

### Content

**Kicker:**

BRAND WORLD

**Headline:**

A retreat for quiet days, open spaces, and natural rhythm.

**Body:**

Riverie brings together the warmth of a tropical villa and the clarity of a boutique stay. Sunlit rooms, shared dining, poolside stillness, and natural textures create a slower way to experience Bali.

**Detail labels:**

OPEN-AIR LIVING / NATURE-FORWARD / QUIET LUXURY / SOULFUL SPACES

### Design Notes

- Keep this section short.
- Do not over-explain the brand story too early.
- This section should create understanding, not become an About page.

---

## Section 04: Stay Preview

### Purpose

Help visitors understand available room or retreat options and move toward room details.

### Recommended Layout

- 2-3 featured room cards.
- Use large imagery and practical text.
- Avoid equal, generic hotel cards if possible. Use an editorial stagger or alternating image sizes.

### Required Card Content

- Room or suite name.
- Short emotional description.
- Size.
- Occupancy.
- Bed type.
- Key amenity or view.
- CTA: View Details or Check Availability.

### Example Card

**River View Suite**

Wake to soft light and quiet views, with a calm room designed for slow mornings and restful evenings.

Details:

- Sleeps 2
- King bed
- Pool or landscape view
- Private bathroom
- Work-friendly corner

CTA:

View Details

### Design Notes

- Use Manrope for practical details.
- Do not hide the important information behind hover states.
- Make the image useful, not only decorative.

---

## Section 05: Experience Highlights

### Purpose

Communicate what makes Riverie different beyond the room.

### Recommended Layout

- 4-6 feature items with thin line icons.
- Can sit below a large image or beside a feature image.

### Feature Set

1. **Open-Air Living**  
   Designed to move between shade, breeze, and natural light.

2. **Nature Connected**  
   Surrounded by greenery, warm textures, and calming views.

3. **Water at Heart**  
   Pool reflections, quiet afternoons, and the gentle flow of life.

4. **Soulful Spaces**  
   Thoughtfully designed spaces for rest, gathering, and connection.

5. **Local Touches**  
   Handcrafted details and Balinese warmth in every moment.

6. **Quiet Luxury**  
   Understated elegance rooted in comfort and authenticity.

### Design Notes

- Icons should be simple, geometric, thin-lined, and no larger than 32px.
- Do not use playful icons or colorful illustration sets.

Let the visitor imagine themselves inside the stay.

### Recommended Layout

- Large editorial image paired with short poetic copy.
- Use poolside, open-air dining, hammock, bedroom view, or morning coffee imagery.

### Content Option

**Kicker:**

CAMPAIGN DIRECTION

**Headline:**

Moments that stay with you.

**Supporting lines:**

- Slow mornings
- Open-air spaces
- Local touches

### Design Notes

## Section 07: Materiality / Atmosphere

### Purpose

Bring the brand guideline into the website by showing the textures and details that define the property.

### Recommended Layout

- Material strip or editorial grid.
- Combine close-up photos with short labels.

### Materials to Feature

- Lime plaster
- Teak wood
- Linen texture
- Water reflections
- Natural stone
- Tropical foliage
- Crafted details

### Design Notes

- This section supports premium perception.
- Use sparingly if the homepage is already image-heavy.
- Avoid fake texture backgrounds unless they are subtle and high quality.

---

## Section 08: Location Context

### Purpose

Reduce uncertainty. Tell guests where Riverie is, what kind of environment it sits in, and what the travel context feels like.

### Recommended Layout

- Simple text and map or location image.
- Avoid cluttered map UI if it breaks the brand.
- Include practical travel cues.

### Content Structure

**Kicker:**

LOCATION

**Headline:**

Close enough to explore, quiet enough to retreat.

**Body:**

Set within Bali’s tropical rhythm, Riverie gives guests a calm base for slow mornings, poolside afternoons, and easy access to local experiences.

**Practical details to add when available:**

- Area or neighborhood.
- Airport travel estimate.
- Nearby beaches, cafes, wellness, nature, or cultural points.
- Transport recommendation.

### Design Notes

- Be specific when real location details are available.
- Do not invent travel times.

---

## Section 09: Journal / Local Notes

### Purpose

Build brand depth and organic SEO through useful editorial content.

### Recommended Layout

- 2-3 article cards.
- Use calm imagery and practical titles.

### Content Themes

- Slow travel in Bali.
- Best quiet mornings near the retreat.
- Design notes from Riverie.
- Local food, cafes, nature, and culture.
- How to plan a restful long stay.

### Design Notes

- If there is no journal content yet, replace this with a short "Local Touches" or "From the Retreat" section.
- Avoid generic SEO filler.

---

## Section 10: Social Proof / Trust

### Purpose

Help visitors feel safe booking.

### Possible Content

- Guest review snippets.
- Press mentions.
- Host note.
- Safety, cleanliness, and service highlights.
- Real guest photos only if brand-appropriate.

### Design Notes

- Keep reviews calm and minimal.
- Avoid large star-rating blocks that feel like a booking marketplace.
- Use review proof only when real reviews exist.

---

## Section 11: Final CTA

### Purpose

Close the homepage with a clear next action.

### Recommended Layout

- Espresso or Limestone background.
- Simple headline, short body, primary CTA, secondary contact path.

### Content Option

**Headline:**

Stay a little longer.

**Body:**

Find your room, choose your dates, and arrive into a slower rhythm in Bali.

**Primary CTA:**

Check Availability

**Secondary CTA:**

Contact Us

### Design Notes

- Do not introduce new information here.
- Keep it focused on action.

### Required Footer Content

- Riverie logo.
- Short brand line.
- Address or area.
- Contact email.
- Phone or WhatsApp.

- Navigation links.

## 6. Homepage Visual Rules

### Color

- Base: Coconut White.
- Section contrast: Limestone.
- Text: Espresso.
- Accent: Teak Brown for hover and small details.
- Palm Green and Pool Blue should appear mostly through imagery.

### Typography

- Hero and emotional section headlines use Playfair Display.
- Practical content uses Manrope.
- Labels and buttons use uppercase Manrope with letter-spacing.
- Do not use long uppercase paragraphs.

### Layout

- Use asymmetric editorial compositions.
- Avoid generic centered SaaS hero layouts.
- Avoid crowded hotel-template room grids.
- Use generous vertical spacing.
- On mobile, stack content clearly and keep CTAs reachable.

### Imagery

Use:

- Pool reflections.
- Palm shadows.
- Open-air dining.
- Calm bedroom views.
- Teak, linen, stone, plaster, and foliage details.
- Human presence only if it feels natural and calm.

Avoid:

- Party scenes.
- Beach-club energy.
- Over-saturated stock tropical photos.
- Influencer-first posing.
- Heavy filters.
- Busy images behind important text.

---

## 7. Booking and Conversion Rules

The homepage must make booking easy, but not aggressive.

### Required CTA Placement

- Hero CTA.
- Booking bar near top of page.
- Room card CTAs.
- Mid-page experience or stay CTA.
- Final CTA before footer.
- Persistent Book Now in navigation.

### CTA Language

Preferred:

- Book Now
- Check Availability
- View Rooms
- Explore the Retreat
- Contact Us

Avoid:

- Hurry, limited rooms.
- Book before it’s gone.
- Massive discount.
- Don’t miss out.

### Trust Cues

Include where available:

- Clear room details.
- Amenities.
- Location context.
- Contact path.
- Cancellation policy.
- Secure booking note.
- Real reviews.

---

## 8. Accessibility Requirements

- Body text should be at least 16px.
- Important text must meet WCAG 2.2 AA contrast.
- Do not place essential small text over photos.
- All CTAs must have visible focus states.
- Booking form must be usable by keyboard.
- Date picker must communicate selected, unavailable, and error states beyond color.
- Images must include useful alt text.
- Motion must respect `prefers-reduced-motion`.
- On mobile, avoid tiny uppercase labels for important details.

---

## 9. SEO and Content Notes

The homepage should include clear, crawlable phrases related to the actual offer.

Use natural phrases such as:

- modern tropical retreat in Bali
- boutique stay in Bali
- tropical villa retreat
- quiet Bali accommodation
- open-air living in Bali
- long-stay retreat in Bali
- calm poolside stay in Bali

Do not stuff keywords. Keep SEO text human, specific, and useful.

### Suggested SEO Title

Riverie, Modern Tropical Retreat in Bali

### Suggested Meta Description

Riverie is a modern tropical retreat in Bali with open-air architecture, quiet rooms, natural textures, poolside spaces, and a slower way to stay.
