#### Overall Impression
The design has exceptional typographic and spatial foundations, but suffers from rhythm and background monotony. The room cards fail to pop because they are Coconut White sitting on a Coconut White background, surrounded by empty spaces.

#### What's Working
1. **Typography Pairing**: The elegant transition from Playfair Display serifs to clean Manrope sans-serifs looks highly editorial.
2. **Interactive Datepicker**: The booking flow features a custom-built, brand-aligned calendar input without relying on standard HTML inputs.

#### Priority Issues
* **[P1] Section Rhythm Monotony**: The Booking Bar, Brand Introduction, and Stay Preview sections share the same white background, causing a flat, untextured visual block.
  * *Why it matters*: Users lose spatial context and the site loses its high-end magazine feel.
  * *Fix*: Alternating backgrounds using the Limestone tone (`var(--limestone)`) to give visual weight to sections.
  * *Suggested command*: `impeccable colorize`
* **[P1] Suite Card Contrast**: Coconut White room cards sitting on a Coconut White background have poor boundary definition.
  * *Why it matters*: It looks unpolished and flat; cards should appear as distinct, layered objects.
  * *Fix*: Set the Stay Preview section background to Limestone (`var(--limestone)`) so the Coconut White cards stand out.
  * *Suggested command*: `impeccable layout`

#### Persona Red Flags
* **Alex (Power User)**: Looking for visual cues of suite premiumness. The lack of card contrast makes the suites look less luxurious, risking early exit.
* **Jordan (First-Timer)**: Scrolling through the brand introduction. The monotony of the background causes them to scroll past the text without reading, missing the key brand ethos.

#### Minor Observations
* The Seseh Valley map is styled beautifully in Limestone but sits directly next to white elements, drawing all the visual weight to the lower sections.

#### Questions to Consider
* What if we treated the background as a series of alternating sand and limestone planes to emulate the shifting light in Bali?
